Nasi Goreng

Description

Nasi Goreng is a traditional Indonesian fried rice dish that is widely popular across Southeast Asia. Made from day-old cooked rice stir-fried with a blend of spices, sweet soy sauce (kecap manis), and various toppings, it delivers a savory, smoky, and slightly sweet flavor that makes it one of the most beloved comfort foods in the region.

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice (day-old)
  • Egg
  • Garlic
  • Shallots
  • Red chili
  • Kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooking oil
  • Green onion
  • Cucumber (for serving)
  • Fried shallots (for topping)

Step

  1. Prepare the Aromatics Finely chop garlic, shallots, and red chili. Set aside. Make sure the cooked rice is cold and not clumpy — day-old rice works best.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics Heat oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. Add garlic, shallots, and chili. Stir-fry for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and golden.
  3. Fry the Egg Push aromatics to the side, crack an egg into the pan, and scramble it quickly. Mix it together with the aromatics.
  4. Add the Rice Add cold cooked rice into the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry over high heat for 2–3 minutes until the rice is well combined with the aromatics.
  5. Season the Rice Add kecap manis, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together until the rice is evenly coated and slightly caramelized. Garnish with:
    • Sliced green onion
    • Fried shallots
    • Sliced cucumber on the side
    • Fried egg on top (optional)
    • Prawn crackers (optional)
